# Workflow-Linked Support Chat Follow-Ups

Canonical workflow ID used in this demo pack: `demo-ai-pm-workflow`

These examples are intentionally written as concise portfolio artifacts. The live app may answer with slightly different wording, but the underlying pattern should stay the same: **run workflow first, then use support chat to explain, refine, and extend the saved analysis**.

## 1. Explain why the top gap matters

### User Follow-Up

Why is the AI platform strategy gap the most important risk for this role?

### Expected Answer Shape

The agent should explain that the role is not only about shipping AI features. It is about making platform-level decisions on trust, admin controls, rollout readiness, and model-powered workflow value. The candidate already looks strong on PM fundamentals, so the biggest hiring risk becomes whether the panel believes she can think like a true enterprise GenAI platform PM.

### Good Signals In The Answer

- References the saved `decisionSummary` and `priorityGaps`
- Connects the gap directly to hiring confidence
- Explains why this matters more than general PM capability
- Ends with a specific prep or positioning action

## 2. Turn the top gaps into a 7-day prep plan

### User Follow-Up

Turn my top gaps into a focused 7-day prep plan for this AI PM interview.

### Expected Answer Shape

The agent should produce a structured week-long plan. A strong answer uses:

- Day 1-2 for AI PM positioning and narrative cleanup
- Day 3-4 for governance, trust, and technical fluency preparation
- Day 5 for resume/story refinement
- Day 6-7 for mock interview practice across product judgment and executive communication

### Good Signals In The Answer

- Uses the saved `interviewPrep.prepPlan` and `gapAnalysis.priorityGaps`
- Prioritizes the highest-risk gaps first
- Keeps the plan realistic for an interview sprint
- Includes what to rehearse, not only what to read

## 3. Rewrite the candidate positioning

### User Follow-Up

Help me frame my background so I sound like a stronger Senior AI Product Manager candidate.

### Expected Answer Shape

The agent should not invent new experience. It should reframe existing experience around:

- enterprise workflow value
- cross-functional product leadership
- AI product judgment
- trust, rollout, and admin-control instincts
- readiness to grow from AI-assisted feature ownership into deeper platform ownership

### Good Signals In The Answer

- Uses the saved `candidateAnalysis.strengths` and `gapAnalysis.positioningAdvice`
- Preserves credibility instead of exaggerating GenAI depth
- Produces language that could be reused in a resume summary or interview intro

## 4. Mock interview questions for the riskiest areas

### User Follow-Up

Give me mock interview questions for the riskiest areas in this workflow.

### Expected Answer Shape

The agent should generate questions around:

- AI product judgment
- enterprise trust and governance
- technical fluency for model-powered systems
- cross-functional leadership under ambiguity

### Good Signals In The Answer

- Builds from the saved `interviewPrep.behavioralQuestionPrompts`
- Anchors questions to the actual risk areas in the workflow
- Includes what a strong answer would likely need to cover

## 5. Explain how to improve the resume for this role

### User Follow-Up

How should I rewrite my resume summary and top bullets for this role?

### Expected Answer Shape

The agent should recommend:

- a sharper AI PM summary line
- stronger emphasis on enterprise AI workflow outcomes
- explicit mention of experimentation, trust, and admin-tooling work
- lighter but more precise technical language around AI systems

### Good Signals In The Answer

- Uses the saved `resumeDefensePoints` and `supportCapabilities`
- Focuses on positioning, not fabrication
- Produces output that can be pasted into a resume draft
